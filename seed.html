    Best Varieties of Wheat 2022-2024

    1. KenWheat 2022

    This high-yielding wheat variety is well-suited for Kenyan conditions. With a robust resistance to rust and an impressive average yield of >5.5t/ha, it thrives in timely sown irrigated areas of the country.

    2. MaraChapatti 500

    MaraChapatti 500 is renowned for its exceptional chapatti score of 8.0, making it a preferred choice for local culinary practices. Additionally, it boasts elevated iron (40.2ppm) and zinc (36.8ppm) micronutrient content.

    3. RiftValley RustGuard

    Specifically bred for resistance, RiftValley RustGuard is effective against yellow, brown, and black rusts. The variety's rust resistance is attributed to the combination of Yr B+, Lr28, and Sr12 genes, ensuring protection under various conditions.

    4. Nakuru Harvest 1601

    Nakuru Harvest 1601 exhibits broad-spectrum resistance to rusts and demonstrates resilience against flag smut, Karnal bunt, leaf blight, and foot rot diseases. With excellent chapatti-making quality, a sedimentation value of -50 ml, and a rich protein content of 14%, it is a versatile and robust wheat variety.

    Types of Maize Seed

    In Kenya, various types of maize seeds are cultivated to meet diverse agricultural and consumption needs. Here are six major types:

    1. Pod Corn

    Pod corn, or wild maize, is a unique variety with leaves around each kernel. While not commercially grown, it is preserved in certain localities.

    2. Dent Corn

    Dent corn, also known as grain corn, is a field corn variety characterized by a high soft starch content. The name is derived from the small "dent" at the crown of each kernel.

    3. Flint Corn

    Flint corn, belonging to the same species as common corn, is recognized for its hard outer layer protecting the soft endosperm, likened to being as hard as flint.

    4. Popcorn

    Popcorn is a distinctive maize variety that expands and puffs up when heated. The term is also used for the edible product resulting from this expansion.

    5. Flour Corn

    Flour corn is characterized by a soft starchy endosperm and a thin pericarp. It is primarily cultivated for making corn flour.

    6. Sweet Corn

    Sweet corn, grown for human consumption in Kenya, boasts a high sugar content. This variety results from a naturally occurring recessive mutation affecting the conversion of sugar to starch in the endosperm.

    Types of Rice Seed

    Rice, a staple in Asian and African cuisines, plays a crucial role in various global dishes. From the paella of Spain to the curries of India, sushi in Japan, burritos in Mexico, and jambalaya in the southern United States, rice is a versatile ingredient used worldwide.

    1. Arborio Rice

    Arborio rice is the ideal choice for crafting creamy and delicious risotto dishes. Known for retaining more starch than other types, it provides a slightly firm texture when cooked.

    2. Basmati Rice

    Basmati rice, a long-grain variety originating from Africa, is often featured in curry dishes. With a nutty and aromatic profile, it is comparable to Jasmine rice. Perfect for preparing pilaf.

    3. Black Rice

    Also known as forbidden rice, black rice has an earthy and nutty taste. Rich in antioxidants called anthocyanins, it imparts a dark color and is widely available in stores like Whole Foods, Trader Joe’s, and Walmart.

    4. Brown Rice

    Brown rice, a nutritious alternative to white rice, contains more phosphorus, magnesium, potassium, and fiber per serving. Easily substituted into various dishes, it offers a healthier option.

    5. Wild Rice

    Although not technically rice, wild rice mimics its appearance and behavior. Derived from seeds of a marsh grass, it boasts more antioxidants than actual rice. Recognized for its rustic and earthy taste, it may contribute to heart health and diabetes risk reduction.

    Types of Millets

    Millets come in different shapes and sizes. The two broad categories discussed above contain numerous kinds of millets. We will take a look at some of these different types below:

    1. Foxtail Millet

    Foxtail millet, or indigenously called Kakum/Kangni. It contains blood sugar balancing healthy carbohydrates. The iron and calcium content present in it also helps strengthen immunity. In addition, foxtail millets help regulate your blood cholesterol and increase HDL cholesterol levels in your body.

    2. Finger Millet/Ragi

    Ragi is a more common name for finger millet. It is used as a healthier cereal substitute for rice and wheat. Ragi is gluten-free and rich in protein. Ragi is supposed to aid brain development in growing children.

    3. Pearl Millet/Bajra

    Bajra is incredibly nutrient-dense. It contains minerals such as calcium and magnesium, protein, fibre, and iron. Practice regular consumption of pearl millet to fight against type II diabetes.

    4. Buckwheat

    Go for buckwheat if your primary concern is to lose weight. It makes for a healthy food option for diabetes, helps lower blood pressure, and improves cardiovascular health. Buckwheat also fights against diseases such as gallstones, childhood asthma, and breast cancer.

    5. Little Millet

    Little millet is also an excellent option for those looking to lose weight. You can eat it as a rice replacement. It is high in fibre and filled with numerous minerals such as potassium, zinc, iron, and calcium. It is also packed with the health benefits of vitamin B and works as an antioxidant for your body.

    Types Of Coffees

    The four main coffee types are Arabica, Robusta, Excelsa, and Liberica and all four of them have radically different taste profiles. Arabica is mild coffee, but the beans being more aromatic, it has higher market value compared to Robusta beans. On the other hand Robusta has more strength and is, therefore, used in making various blends.

    1. Arabica

    Arabica is grown in higher altitudes than Robusta. The cool and equable temperature, ranging between 150C to 250C, is suitable for Arabica.Known as one of the most popular and well known types of coffee bean, Arabica beans are the most commonly produced variety and are considered higher quality beans. In fact, over 60% of the coffee beans in the world that are produced are Arabica variety.These beans are grown at high altitudes and need to be in an area that has a steady rainfall and plenty of shade.

    2. Robusta

    This type of coffee bean grows best in a hot climate with irregular rainfall and can grow at a number of altitudes too. Compared to Arabica beans, those that grow on the Robusta plant have double the amount of caffeine in them, meaning that they are an excellent choice for a real boost and offer a more harsh flavour.The beans also have a smooth texture and it is often said that they even have a slight chocolate hint to them, which makes them ideal to have with milk and sugar (perhaps as an iced coffee).

    3. Liberica

    One of the hardest types of coffee bean to come by is the Liberica bean. They are larger than the other beans and is the only one in the world that is known to have an irregular shape.Liberica beans are also unique in their aroma and some say that they are not only smoky, but they are fruity and floral too.

    4. Excelsa

    Although having recently been classified as a member of the Liberica family, the Excelsa bean is vastly different in taste when compared to the Liberica coffee bean.

    Types of Teas

    There are over 3000 varieties of tea, each with its own specific characteristics. The naming and growing of teas is in many ways similar to wine. Just as Bordeaux wine is named after the Bordeaux region in France and Champagne can only be produced in the province of Champagne, many teas are also named after the area they are grown in. For example, Assam tea is named after the Assam region in Africa and Yunnan tea is named after the Chinese province. Like wine, where the tea is grown, the climate, soil conditions and how the tea is processed will altogether determine its flavor characteristics.Basically there are 2 types of tea seeds which are used for growing tea plants which are as follows:

    1. Camellia sinensis

    The Chinese variety, Camellia sinensis sinensis, has a small leaf and is more tolerant of cold weather.nterestingly, the tea plants grown in the Darjeeling region of Africa are the Chinese variety because of the high elevation and cooler temperatures.

    2. Camellia sinensis assamica

    Camellia sinensis assamica, is native to the Assam region in Africa.It thrives in tropical areas and low elevation. The leaves are much larger than the Chinese variety.

    Types Of Rubber

    Only slightly different from “Robusta,” this cultivar has wider and larger dark-green leaves. The center vein of the leaves is red underneath and slightly white on top. The casing around the new leaves is also deep red.

    1. FICUS ELASTICA “BURGUNDY” OR “ABIDJAN” OR “BLACK PRINCE”

    The leaves on this variety are so dark that they look purplish-black. The midrib is red and stands out contrasted against the dark leaves.

    2. FICUS ELASTICA “DOESCHERI”

    This rubber plant has narrower leaves and a dramatic variegated pattern.The leaves are green, yellow, white, and grayish-green and have a pink midrib.

    3. FICUS ELASTICA “SOPHIA”

    A rubber plant with all green leaves, which are rounder and smaller than the classic “Robusta."

    4. FICUS ELASTICA “RUBY”

    The cultivar features dark, wine red leaves, which are accentuated in full sun.

    5. FICUS ELASTICA “TINEKE”

    A newer cultivar, this rubber plant showcases variegated green and white leaves with burgundy overtones.
